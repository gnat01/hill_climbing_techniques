# Codes in Python and R

## What Can Be Coded

### Python

- A pedagogical LK-lite implementation for Euclidean TSP.
- Supporting routines for candidate edge sets, gain computation, and feasibility checks.
- Comparisons against 2-opt and simulated annealing on common instances.
- Tour-geometry visualizations before and after major improving moves.

### R

- Smaller-scale educational versions emphasizing the gain sequence logic.
- Benchmark notebooks contrasting fixed-depth and variable-depth local search.
- Diagnostic plots on move depth and gain distributions.

## Extensions

- Candidate sets based on nearest neighbors for speed.
- Modern LK variants and Lin-Kernighan-Helsgaun style refinements.
- Integration as a local-search kernel inside SA, ILS, or GA hybrids.
- Asymmetric and constrained routing adaptations.
- Parallel multi-start LK with elite-solution tracking.
